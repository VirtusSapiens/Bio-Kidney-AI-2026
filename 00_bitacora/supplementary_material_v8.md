## S1. Figure Legends

### Figure 1. Three-dimensional rendering of the CCO v8 renal vascular tree.

Complete vascular architecture generated by the Constrained Constructive Optimization algorithm (v8), rendered in Blender from `renal_data_v1.json`. The model comprises 1,902 segments distributed across three systems: arterial (red, 904 segments), venous (blue, 926 segments), and collecting (yellow, 72 segments). Vessel caliber is proportional to segment radius, ranging from 500 µm at the main renal artery to 38 µm at terminal arterioles. The translucent ellipsoidal wireframe represents the kidney boundary (11.0 x 6.0 x 5.0 cm). Green points indicate 1,300 glomerular demand seeds generated with a Beta(3, 1.2) radial distribution biased toward the cortex, with 63.1% falling in the cortical zone (normalized radius > 0.60). Note the visibly higher vascular density in the peripheral cortical region compared to the medullary zone, reflecting the anatomical distribution of nephrons in the human kidney. Hiliar markers: red sphere = arterial hilum, blue sphere = venous hilum, gold sphere = renal pelvis. All bifurcations satisfy Murray's Law (alpha = 3.0, 100% compliance, 915/915 verified). Rendering performed with Principled BSDF materials and subsurface scattering for organic tissue appearance. Scale bar: 10 mm.

### Figure 2. Agentic AI workflow: from computational instruction to bioprinted kidney model.

Schematic representation of the end-to-end pipeline employed in Bio-Kidney AI 2026. The workflow is organized in six sequential stages: **(A)** Natural-language specification of biophysical requirements in the development environment (VS Code + Claude Code CLI on Ubuntu Linux, 16 GB RAM); **(B)** AI-assisted code generation and iterative refinement of six simulation modules — CCO vascular tree, Krogh oxygen diffusion, iPSC kinetics, Co-SWIFT bioprinting, Starling-Deen glomerular filtration, and Kedem-Katchalsky tubular reabsorption; **(C)** Constrained Constructive Optimization generating 1,902 vascular segments with Murray's Law compliance, Beta(3, 1.2) cortical demand distribution, and two-pass calibrated Poiseuille pressure assignment; **(D)** Structured data export to `renal_data_v1.json` containing per-segment coordinates (mm), diameters (µm), and inlet/outlet pressures (mmHg); **(E)** Blender import via batch geometry script (single mesh, 15,216 vertices, 7,608 quads) producing publication-quality 3D visualization; **(F)** Functional validation yielding bilateral GFR = 115.2 mL/min with terminal capillary pressures of 58.6 +/- 13.4 mmHg. Arrows indicate data flow direction. Dashed borders denote AI-assisted steps. The complete pipeline executes in under 60 seconds on consumer hardware, demonstrating that physiologically calibrated organ-scale vascular modeling is achievable without high-performance computing infrastructure.

### Figure 3. Comparative glomerular filtration rate: CCO v7 baseline vs. v8 calibrated model.

Bar chart comparing bilateral GFR estimates between the v7 baseline and v8 calibrated vascular models. **(A)** Left panel: v7 baseline with uniform demand distribution (1,000 points) and synthetic pressure model produced a GFR estimate of approximately 82 mL/min/1.73 m² (CKD Stage 2 equivalent, approximately 70% of normal function). Under naive Poiseuille pressure assignment, terminal pressures collapsed to 20 mmHg, yielding GFR = 0 mL/min (red dashed line). **(B)** Right panel: v8 calibrated model with cortically-biased demand (1,300 points, Beta(3, 1.2)) and two-pass Poiseuille calibration produced a bilateral GFR of 115.2 mL/min (57.6 mL/min per kidney), falling within the normal physiological range of 100-125 mL/min (green shaded band). The horizontal dashed lines indicate clinical thresholds: 90 mL/min (CKD Stage 1/2 boundary), 60 mL/min (CKD Stage 2/3 boundary), and 43 mmHg minimum capillary pressure for net filtration. Inset: Starling pressure decomposition showing P_gc = 58.6 mmHg, P_Bowman = 15.0 mmHg, pi_gc = 28.0 mmHg, yielding net driving pressure Delta_P = 15.6 mmHg. Error bars on the v8 estimate represent +/- 1 SD of terminal pressure variability (13.4 mmHg). The 40.2% increase from v7 synthetic estimate to v8 calibrated GFR is attributable entirely to the hemodynamic model improvement, not to changes in filtration parameters.

## S4. Data Availability

All vascular data are exported to `renal_data_v1.json` (841 KB) containing:
- 1,902 per-segment records with 3D coordinates, diameters, pressures, and terminal status
- 579 arterial terminal entries with glomerular capillary pressure
- Global statistics (coverage, Murray compliance, segment counts)
- Flat arrays (`radii_um`, `pressures_kpa`) for pipeline compatibility

Companion CSV: `arbol_vascular_cco_v8.csv` (14 columns, 1,902 rows).
